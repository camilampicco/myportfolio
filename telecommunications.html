PRM Redesign for Client B

Unifying two core businesses into a single streamlined PRM for a leading telecommunications company.

Context

Client B, a telecommunications company, manages two core business areas. Each had its own PRM (Partner Relationship Management) system, but this created inefficiencies: duplicated content, outdated information, and a fragmented user experience. To solve this, the company decided to merge both PRMs into a single unified platform.

Challenge

The original PRM hosted both business units in visibly separated sections, leading to redundancy and confusion. The redesign needed to:

  • Consolidate all content into one streamlined system
  • Preserve clarity between Core Business 1 and Core Business 2
  • Improve usability and efficiency for internal teams and external partners

Approach

  • Analyzed traffic and data over a full calendar year
  • Redesigned the PRM with a unified navigation menu
  • Built the Assets page from scratch, in collaboration with developers and business managers
  • Conducted iterative user testing with end-users
  • Followed a full design thinking process

Outcomes & Impact

  • Reduced redundancy by removing outdated and duplicate content
  • Simplified access to information with unified navigation
  • Maintained clarity between two business units while merging platforms
  • Improved efficiency for both partners and internal teams
